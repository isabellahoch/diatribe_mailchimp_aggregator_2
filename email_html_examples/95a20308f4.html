In his article on “Time-in-Range, What’s An Achievable Goal with Diabetes?” Adam shares his approach to time-in-range goals and his key strategies to spend more time-in-range each day. Adam describes how focusing on the “process” of keeping glucose in range each day helps him stay within a tighter range, particularly by identifying and replicating his bright spots. Read more of his tips here.   
 
Speaking of bright spots, have you heard of our “Be a Bright Spot” campaign? This new campaign allows diaTribe readers to directly sponsor copies of Bright Spots & Landmines for people with diabetes, educators, and healthcare providers across the country! You can Be a Bright Spot through a $25 donation that will immediately help three people in the diabetes community. Thank you for helping us get Bright Spots & Landmines into the hands of more people with diabetes who are seeking actionable advice and help in managing their diabetes.
 
2017 was an incredible year for CGM, with the approval of Abbott’s FreeStyle Libre, Medicare reimbursement for Dexcom's G5 CGM, the popularization of the “AGP” standardized report, and more user-friendly CGM data. With these advances, what should time-in-range goals be for people lucky enough to wear CGM? We recently interviewed 14 experts and distilled their key insights on individualizing time-in-range goals and increasing access to CGM. More here.  
 
Our diaTribe Series on Access continues with a new article on “Maintaining Diabetes Care While Changing Insurance.” To help make the process of changing insurance easier for people with diabetes, we have put together a step-by-step guide to maintaining care during transitions, whether changing jobs, turning 26, or moving to a new state. Read about options for filling the gap between old and new coverage and other helpful tips here.
 
Finally, diabetes tech moves so quickly, and we’re excited to share an overview of “Diabetes Tech on the Horizon – New Insulin Delivery Systems Coming in 2018.” In this article, we provide a list of new insulin delivery devices, pumps, and pens that have recently launched or are expected to launch in 2018.
