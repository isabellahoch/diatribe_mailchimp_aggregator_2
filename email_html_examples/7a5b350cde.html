I'm also writing to express happiness today. As many have no doubt seen, there was very exciting news late last week that continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) with insulin-dosing claims, like Dexcom's G5, finally have a path to Medicare reimbursement. In the coming months, Dexcom will work with CMS to implement coverage, enabling those on Medicare to finally receive CGM reimbursement. This news is a major win for hundreds of thousands of people with diabetes covered under Medicare, for whom severe hypoglycemia is more likely and for whom CGM could be life-saving. Thank you to everyone who worked so hard to help achieve this critical milestone, including all 10,000 patients and families who signed the petition The diaTribe Foundation created last summer! We were very inspired by the JDRF and their incredible work over years that led to this outcome and we also salute our very own Adam Brown who gave a most compelling and persuasive address (see his slides) to the FDA. The diaTribe Foundation also covered the costs to bring nearly a dozen patients to this meeting and we thank so many of you for your help that enables diaTribe to take on outsized goals like this to amplify patient voice. My presentation at that meeting discussed the opinions of hundreds of people on CGM and the talk by our head of Advocacy, Ava Runge's, focused on her and her father's experience with CGM - we believe so many varied voices contributed to the decision on this front to allow insulin dosing using CGM - and that, of course, was central to this Medicare progress.
