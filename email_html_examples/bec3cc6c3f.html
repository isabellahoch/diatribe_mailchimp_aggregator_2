1. Low-Carb, High-Impact – 22 Diabetes Recipes
Popular food columnist Catherine Newman is back with a series of low-carb recipes (22 in total!) that can help people with diabetes maintain stable blood sugars. In her article, she shares creative low-carb alternatives to traditional favorites, including cottage cheese pancakes, cauliflower rice, and herby Asian-style lettuce wraps. You’ll find step-by-step recipes for these healthy, delicious meals here.
 
2. FDA Clears Dexcom G6 CGM with No Fingersticks! Launch Later this Year
If you haven’t yet heard the big news, the FDA cleared the Dexcom G6 real-time CGM with no fingerstick calibrations. The G6 was submitted to the FDA just six months, and it is expected to launch later this year. The G6 also comes with a one-button insertion device, longer 10-day wear time, and approval for ages 2 years and older. Read more about this tremendous win here.
 
3. Two Pills for Type 1 Diabetes Under US and European Review
For the first time ever, two pills for type 1 diabetes are being considered by the European Medicines Agency (EMA) and Food and Drug Administration (FDA). Farxiga (a once-daily SGLT-2 inhibitor in pill form) is under review by the EMA, and sotagliflozin (an SGLT-1/SGLT-2 dual inhibitor) is under review by both the EMA and FDA. These drugs, which work independently from insulin, have been shown to lower insulin dose requirements, contribute to weight loss, and reduce highs and lows. More here!
 
4. How to Change Any Diabetes Habit, Part 1: Environment Beats Self-Control
Can you guess what a vacuum cleaner taught Adam about his diabetes habits? In his new Adam’s Corner, Adam explores the impact of environment on behavior: moving his vacuum cleaner to the kitchen helps him vacuum more, and keeping his glucose meter next to bed helps him check his blood sugar before sleeping and after waking up. In his article, he shares a plethora of other easy environmental changes that help him maintain healthy habits like using a smaller dinner plate to eat less food, putting his phone on airplane mode from 9 pm to 9 am, and keeping junk food out of sight. Read more of his tips here.
