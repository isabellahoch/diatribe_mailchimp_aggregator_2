When life is busy and schedules are packed, exercise is often the first activity to go. In Adam’s article, he discusses the dangers of the “All or Nothing” exercise mentality – an all-too-common assumption that you have to take an hour out of a busy day to exercise or do nothing at all. Adam reframes his mindset by using a mental trick: “Five minutes of activity beats zero minutes.” Read more of his tips for dodging the “All of Nothing” landmine here.
  • “The Latest in Diabetes Drugs: Less Hypoglycemia, More Weight Loss” by Emma Ryan  
Curious about which diabetes therapies are making news this fall? Our article shares the latest updates and newest research from the recent European Association for the Study of Diabetes (EASD) meeting. Click here for more on diabetes therapies that are helping lower A1c, increase “time in zone,” support heart health, promote weight loss, and decrease hypoglycemia – including semaglutide, Bydureon, Tresiba, and more!
 
  • “Dexcom Submits G6 Sensor to FDA” by Ben Pallant and Adam Brown 

We covered Dexcom’s submission of its next-gen G6 CGM sensor for FDA approval in this New Now Next. The G6 sensor requires one finger stick calibration per day, can be worn for ten days, has a 30% thinner transmitter, and features a redesigned one-button inserter. To learn about what else is coming up in Dexcom’s pipeline – like disposable CGMs and integration with Fitbit’s Ionic smartwatch – click here.
