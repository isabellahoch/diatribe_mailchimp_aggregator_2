Dear Dennis, Jeff, Orville, and Ruth –


Our team sends its warmest summer greetings your way!
 
Thank you SO incredibly much again for sharing your valuable time and invaluable wisdom with us at last month’s Board Meeting. Can you believe that was nearly a month ago already?!
 
We’ve been firing on all cylinders here at our Haight Street office, preparing for not one, not two, but THREE pivotal events for our beloved Foundation in as many months – not to mention keeping up with all of our regular goings on, too! We’re thrilled for (and so very busy working toward) what we’re sure will be some momentous convenings for the entire diabetes ecosystem: this Friday’s conference on “Glycemic Outcomes Beyond A1C” in Maryland (!), our annual “Solvable Problems in Diabetes” gathering at EASD (this year in Lisbon, Portugal), and, of course, d17!

With so much on the go, it truly seems unfathomable that only three short years ago we were a fledgling nonprofit that raised (what now seems like a paltry) $80K. We have big obstacles, even bigger goals, and the biggest of beliefs that we can and will positively impact the diabetes, prediabetes, and obesity landscape. There’s so much to be done, and inspiringly, so much that can be done to improve access to therapies and care, to promote policies that better the health of this nation, to empower regulatory bodies to work alongside patients, to learn and disseminate knowledge about preventing chronic conditions … we could go on and on. Whenever I stop and think I about how far we’ve come in such a short time and all of the big, ambitious goals we have ahead, I am so very filled with gratitude for each and every one of you, for what you’ve brought to the table so far, and for the enormous sources of guidance and support you continue to be – words cannot do justice to just how thankful I am – and, of course, our team is!

It is also worth noting explicitly some challenges! With all our ambitions for the year, a lot of things have to go right on the fundraising front ($350,000 more to raise this year!) and we also have our work cut out for us on engaging patients. After gaining over 40,000 diaTribe subscribers in 2016, we’ve invested less this year and are on track to grow our subscriber list by ~5-10K fewer this year – this is partially to focus more on increasing readership (we want to get back up to ~2 million annual unique site visitors, about a 400K increase from where we’re currently at), but we also want to be thinking about how to ramp up investment and engagement in new and strategic ways to pursue multiple goals at once.
