Due to our shortened board meeting, we were not able to fully introduce you to the AMAZING team of new full-time and summer associates which has joined us in the past few weeks. Here is a little about each of them:
 
Emily Fitts (Amherst '17) hails from Providence, RI. She was an all-star summer associate last summer and was offered a position following her time with us (this is highly unusual). She’s spent a whole summer living in San Francisco and working in our office, so she will be the resident expert on the city in addition to our other full-time associates. Emily is majored in psychology at Amherst and has a Five-College Certificate in Culture, Health, and Science. She worked for the Union for International Cancer Control in Geneva, Switzerland, as an External Relations intern and is also an avid rower.
 
Emma Ryan (Amherst '17) is from Boise, Idaho. At Amherst, she majored in Law, Jurisprudence and Social Thought. She was captain of the varsity crew and studied abroad in Paris. Her interests include the symphony orchestra, backpacking, and skiing.
 
Jeemin Kwon (Yale '17) will be the new Managing Editor for diaTribe! She’s from San Jose, CA, and studied abroad in Seoul, South Korea. She has extensive research experience and is part of a Korean Drum and Dance troupe.
 
Isabel Chin (Brown '17) majored in Public Health and is in the dual degree Medical School Program. Her graduation (with her twin sister, also at Brown) was May 28. She is from Pasadena, CA, and studied abroad in Cuba. Isabel has been living with diabetes for 19 years and developed a curriculum for AYUDA to teach children in Ecuador about the disease. She will be transitioning to dQ&A in the fall but we are so excited to have her with us for the summer!
 
Dalton Price (Cornell ’20) is a major in population health and social justice. He has considerable research experience and holds the Guinness Book of World Records for Most Skateboard Rock and Rolls in 30 Seconds (2011). He will be based largely in Aspen this summer.
 
Julia Cohen (Dartmouth ’18) is an anthropology major and neuroscience minor. She studied abroad in Buenos Aires and taught children in Vermont through a non-profit called "Growing Change.” She will be based largely in Aspen this summer and is our 24th Dartmouth Fellow.
 
Lily Wushanley (Swarthmore ’18), a former extern extraordinaire in early 2017, is a Spanish major with a minor in biology. She recently returned from a semester abroad in Santiago, Chile, where, in addition to perfecting her Spanish, she spent significant time volunteering at a local clinic for malnourished infants.
 
As promised, you can also review the draft agenda for d17 here. If you have any comments or feedback on this agenda, please let us know! We'd love to know your thoughts. 
 
Finally, I wish to extend tremendous thanks to Ruth for her incredible impact on The diaTribe Foundation as one of our Directors. Over these years, you have provided expert guidance, wisdom, and advice to our Foundation, and you have served as an amazing role model for all of the diaTribe associates who have passed through as well as our leaders. Our nonprofit could not have grown and flourished without you, so, from the bottom of our hearts – thank you! We will miss you at our board meetings, but we can’t wait to see you at d17!

Our next board meeting will be held on Monday, September 25 – just a couple of days before d17. We are looking forward to the months ahead, and we will stay in touch with updates on our progress!
 
yours truly,
kelly and The diaTribe Foundation team
