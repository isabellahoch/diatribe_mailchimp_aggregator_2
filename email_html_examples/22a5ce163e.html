SEPTEMBER 28, 2016, TDF MEETING HIGHLIGHTS
 
Budget and Fundraising:
  • TdF is on track to raise a million dollars by the end of 2016 as long as a number of the pledges about which we are optimistic come in - the $75,000 from this morning is very welcome on this front!
  • TdF needs to focus on new funding sources beyond corporate donors. We think this is a matter of lack of time and lack of knowledge rather than a lack of available network or possible funds we could raise.
  • The group identified the value of leveraging the diaTribe.org subscriber list for fundraising. This is the start of a “major donor” list.
  • New events have performed well, and there is an opportunity to sustain and/or even expand Musings in the future (rather than continue putting additional resources into the ADA Forum with TCOYD). While it took well over five years to get to $75,000 in funds raised for the TCOYD event, we reached that our first year for Musings. That said, given our desire to keep the “floor” at $25,000 for Musings, there may not be enormous room to increase this the second year (that said, it will stay far less work!)
  • Helmsley has made it clear that it will not fund “instrastructure needs” for our Foundation beyond 2017; they have indicated they may be open to programming though have consistently expressed a desire to move out of funding “small” nonprofits. Advocacy programs could be a good opportunity, especially those focused on manufacturer/payer relationships and on access.
  • There is a strong potential for a funding package model, whereby sponsors could decide up front (i.e., starting now through the beginning of 2017) what TdF events and programs they would like to fund throughout the year. This would reduce “fatigue” by us contacting them twice or even more about ADA and EASD, which has typically taken place in April or May for ADA (too late) and July for EASD (also too late!). This would reduce work needed on our part and our goal is to get in touch with all manufacturers by year end.
 
Search for a COO/Executive Director:
  • This is critical  to alleviate management constraints at TdF.
  • Kelly and John have been in discussions with Cassie from Common Careers, who will charge $40,000 for her headhunting services. The group determined that the price is worth it to find a quality hire.
  • We are keeping specific title and compensation open for future negotiations.
  • The COO/ED will ideally be full-time and based in SF.
  • Please let us know if you would like to be on the Search Committee. Either way, we of course will keep the board informed on the plan and approach. 
  • The contract will not be signed until the official references are in (requests made today) and we have negotiated what happens if we do not find someone.
 
Advocacy:
  • Lorraine highlighted the importance of a case statement for advocacy-specific fundraising and the group discussed this at length, generating ideas.
  • John and Ava developed an “Advocacy 101” scorecard to direct an ongoing search for best programs for TdF. FDA advocacy has continued and is a strong fit for the foundation. They will send this separately and ask for feedback. 
  • Access is a major area of interest for the group, and the Payer Playbook is an appealing program. We will continue to talk to Lilly and others as well such as HCT - we understand that perceived bias is an issue.
  • Part of the discussion also focused on assessing the potential for healthcare provider-oriented advocacy and education initiatives.
  • Website redesign over the next two to three months will include a new advocacy homepage.
 
diaTribe/Education:
  • The year-end goal of 90,000 subscribers is on track. There is potential for 100k+ subscribers by end of year with additional funding and strategy – John and Lynn will confer on this.
  • diaTribe has reached its peak to date of 2 million unique visitors to the site. We’ll end 2016 closer to 1.7 million most likely – we are hoping to drive an agreement by the end of the year or something similar to boost site traffic and hit the year end stretch goal of 2 million. BD is a top prospect.
  • There is a significant opportunity to leverage diaTribe’s readership for fundraising and for marketing Adam’s book. We learned so much from the board on potential marketing ideas …
 
Adam’s Book:
  • Adam gave us a great presentation where he outlined initial ideas. So far, he proposed that the book would be available online for “name a fair price;” a hard copy will be available on Amazon for a price equivalent to those of other similar hard copy books.
  • Early versions may be sent to pharmaceutical/device manufacturers before full release of the book in return for contributions to TdF – early versions would also be sent to “key opinion leaders” on the scientist, researcher, and blogger fronts.
  • The book could be used to raise donations during TdF’s end-of-year campaign with potential “major donors” who like to be insiders and will love getting an “early look”!.
  • Involving social media and reviews from bloggers is key in raising awareness. There is lots of room for work on Facebook with MediaCause – we’d also get a hashtag on twitter once we have a title!
  • There is potential for sending the book to other countries to promote it; could also translate it into Spanish for US Spanish-speakers. It would be important to get the cultural tone right.
  • Roll out will likely be in December or February.
  • There is potential for promoting or serializing the book through Adam’s columns on diaTribe.
