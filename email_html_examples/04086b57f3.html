Part 3: Education: 

After the exciting end-of-year push to surpass 100K subscribers by Jan. 1 (still so psyched we hit this milestone!!), diaTribe has turned its focus its 2017 strategy. And we’re already on a roll in many respects! Year to date, we have over 300K unique site visitors, putting us on track for 1.7-1.8 million unique site visitors by EOY if we remain on this trajectory. This would be about mid-way through our 1.5 million to 2.0 site number visits that we saw last year but without the enormous corporate boost (we'd do much better if we could get this!). That said, we should expect to see a key driver of site traffic - Google AdGrants - ramp up in the coming months, which should further increase site visits. As we went “all in” for our 2016 EOY subscribers goal, we turned our AdGrants budget fully to subscribers (instead of splitting the budget evenly between subscriber acquisition and driving site traffic). Media Cause previously alerted us that, due to the specifics of the Google search engine algorithm, it would take a few months for our traffic-driving ads to fully regain Google’s trust and receive priority in search results. As our AdGrants ads return to their peak form, our site traffic should continue to rise. The Facebook ad spending was about 10x more efficient than Google's - our Google money is "free (though we do have to re-apply this year) - and we're going to try to see what funds we can get from Facebook in the form of ad spending. 

On the subscriber front, we're at about 103,000 - comfortably above the "milestone" 100,000, but not well above it and have added far fewer subscribers in the first two months of the year this year than last year due to decisions about where to put resources. We've had fewer issues to date (two this year) due to team focus in other areas, and we're not spending as much. We are hopeful about 2017 and eager to discuss goals with the board. Generally speaking, we would like to focus more on engagement than numbers of subscribers though we'd like to agree on goals and metrics for each. 

Big picture, we are very excited about initiatives we’re undertaking to further boost our traffic and impact in 2017. In particular, we are readily preparing to capitalize on opportunities surrounding the launch of both Adam’s book (coming later this spring!) and a freshly redesigned diaTribe.org (stay tuned to see the new designs at the upcoming board meeting!). We are also finalizing the education grant with Lilly noted above that will support a series of diaTribe.org articles focused on patient access and the insurance landscape more broadly. It is exciting to branch out and expand our coverage into areas that we know to be particularly important to people with diabetes, both in the US and around the world. Along the same vein, we have enlisted the expertise of professional author and food guru Catherine Newman to help us create high-quality, original content on diabetes and food, and we are in the process of figuring out how to better cover intersectional topics - like heart health and diabetes - on our website. 

We continue to think strategically about how best to share and promote all content we create so that it can have maximal impact. Very excitingly, we most recently hit a new Facebook milestone: our most successful post ever! Not only did our post promoting a New Now Next article on an early accuracy study of Dexcom’s G6 sensor receive over 127K impressions on Facebook, including over 500 shares, it also has over 21K views on diaTribe.org, with ~60% of that traffic driven through Facebook. Other tech pieces have performed similarly well: our “Tech on the Horizon” piece from Fall 2016 has netted another ~16K views in 2017, the "2016 EOY Closed Loop piece" has another ~10K views YTD, and a "CGM tips" piece published almost one week ago already has ~5K views - great performance for an article a week out of the gates. All in all, we’re seeing some good results for 2017 already, and we’re even more excited for the year ahead!
