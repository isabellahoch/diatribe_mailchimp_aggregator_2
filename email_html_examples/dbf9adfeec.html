With Thanksgiving just behind us, I’ve been reflecting about everything I’m thankful for this year – my family, our diaTribe team, and, of course, all of you and your continuous support of our foundation. Several weeks ago, it was the thirtieth anniversary of my diagnosis with diabetes. With this in mind, I’ve been reflecting on all of the progress that’s been made in the past thirty years, how lucky I am to have access to so much of the progress, and how moved and impressed I am with everyone who is working so relentlessly to make more improvements in the years ahead in therapy, in technology, and in our evolving healthcare system.

This issue of diaTribe features some of these bright spots while also highlighting the work that lies ahead. Here is your special preview of articles that cover the second part of our health insurance navigation series, new BD/Medtronic MiniMed technology (that we share in a Test Drive), the results of a very impressive early accuracy study on Dexcom’s G6 sensor, and much more…

Tech guru Adam has been busy testing the new Medtronic/BD MiniMed Pro-set with FlowSmart, which we both received in the mail just days after the launch of the infusion set in the United States. In our Test Drive, we give you an inside look into what it’s like to insert (very painless!) and wear the set, and we share some things we really relish plus what could be improved.

This is the actionable guide Adam wishes he had been handed at diagnosis, focused on the real-life tactics that have helped improve his diabetes and mental outlook (Diabetes Bright Spots), and the diabetes mistakes he's constantly trying to avoid (Diabetes Landmines). He would LOVE to share a special early preview with you; just email him! I can tell you - I wept after I read it, I thought it was that good. We’ve just published the second part of our series focused on helping people with diabetes navigate the complex health insurance landscape. Last time, we discussed how people with diabetes can pay for their medications and care and we had a lot of very direct feedback that people are glad diaTribe is doing more on this front. This time, we’re covering the ins and outs of searching for the insurance that provides the best coverage, care, and support for someone with diabetes. As we deliberate on the insurance questions that we’ll be tackling in the future, we’d love to hear about the most pressing insurance question YOU have! Email us with your questions and suggestions. 

Finally, the issue also highlights results from an early accuracy study on Dexcom’s longer-wear, less calibration G6 Sensor. A 49-person trial showed that 96% of G6 readings were within 20% or 20 mg/dl of the true glucose value. These results indicate that the G6 will not only have better or equal accuracy to G5, but will also have double the wear duration (10-14 days) - that last part is especially exciting to so many on CGM. The G6 sensor is expected to launch in 2018. This New Now Next also shares details about the bandage-like, “thinnest CGM ever” currently being developed by Dexcom and Verily (first-gen expected to launch late 2018).

And, I hope you and your family had a wonderful Thanksgiving, and I wish you a memorable holiday season ahead.
