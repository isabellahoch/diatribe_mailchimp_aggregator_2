Making Sense of Diabetes

Dear diaTribe reader,
 
As you know, Hurricanes Harvey and Irma in the US and Caribbean have left many people with diabetes without their insulin or other vital supplies including pens, lancets, and strips. Our diaTribe team just spoke to Dr. Mark Atkinson and his wife, Carol, who are the incredible two-person partnership behind Insulin for Life and have been so generous with their time. They are leaving Florida as we write, driving to safety, as we are still in the midst of Hurricane Irma, possibly the most powerful storm recorded over the Atlantic Ocean; its full blast is expected to hit Florida by the end of this weekend. With these communities in mind, we are again calling on anyone who has the means to donate money to Insulin for Life, a 501(c)3 non-profit and recognized pharmaceutical distributor. All gifts to Insulin for Life are tax-deductible and legal – even prescription drugs. See below on insulin and supplies - we heard from Dr. Atkinson today, please do not send supplies until you see the "ok" to do so this coming week. The supplies will be badly needed, and we're just advising to wait 'til the best time to send. We'll be back on social media with specifics - for now, PLEASE consider getting supplies ready to send and/or making a cash donation - even $1, especially $1, will help! Thank you for pulling together as a diabetes community - Dr. Atkinson said they've been receiving 80-100 packages per day and more compared to usually 15 or fewer - your actions are having an impact! 

As a bonus for donating, for the first 10 donors and the 50th donor, we will give away an autographed copy of diaTribe columnist Adam Brown’s new book, “Bright Spots & Landmines: The Diabetes Guide I Wish Someone Had Handed Me.” The book is a smart, empathetic guide on how to manage your diabetes, from someone who’s lived it, knows it, and wants to share it. To donate Insulin:

Please prepare to donate supplies again - Insulin for Life itself is in the path of Hurricane Irma this weekend so please just prepare your supplies to send and keep in the refrigerator until you get the "all clear" to send. As many of you know who have already done this (thank you!!), the insulin must be unopened and non-expired, and it is important to keep the insulin cool while it is in transit. Here are some tips for protecting insulin:

  • Keep insulin refrigerated until shipping the package
  • Place the insulin with a frozen gel pack (or other equivalent) in a Styrofoam container or thermal insulated bag (see the bottom of this webpage for examples)
  • If you don’t have any Styrofoam containers or thermal insulated bags, place the insulin and freezer pack in a zip-closure bag and wrap the bag with bubble wrap
Shipping should be expedited as much as possible, with the time being no longer than two days. Remember, for this weekend, wait to send the package until you hear the all-clear from Insulin for Life or the ADA Coalition. We'll be updating our story on diaTribe.org (see diaTribe.org/hurricane) and so many others on social media will be keeping you up to date as well ! 
  • Once you have the all-clear to send, remember to include a piece of paper with your name and address in the package to receive a receipt acknowledging your tax-deductible donation  
  • Although normally, Insulin for Life advises sending packages early in the week, please stand by for the best information as Hurricane Irma is still going on now (all our best thoughts to those of you there)

In addition to insulin, there is a very urgent need for other diabetes supplies. Insulin for Life is gladly accepting meters, pens, lancets, and strips as well, as well as GLP-1. The diabetes community will let you know when to send again to IFL - it will be any day.
