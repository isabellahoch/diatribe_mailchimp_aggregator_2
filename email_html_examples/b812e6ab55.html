December 9, 2016 TdF Board Meeting Highlights
 
Education
  • The number of diaTribe.org subscribers is approximately 98K as of Dec. 13 and the stretch goal is still 100K by the end of the year; the original goal of 90K has already been reached
  • ~48,000 unique visitors per month  to diaTribe.org in the past six months; ~58,000 unique visitors per month over past three months
  • Tech pieces have driven much of the traffic
  • Every single one of our top ten posts in November this year outperformed the top one post last year
 
Development/End-of-year Fundraising Campaign
  • diaTribe is conducting a lot of A/B testing to determine which kind of messaging works best for fundraising – we are already on our eighth A/B test this year!
  • So far, we’ve learned that standard nonprofit messaging works well, and that advocacy-specific subscribers are particularly engaged
  • Open rates have ranged from 15-32% for emails
  • While click through rates are relatively low, we’ve seen a 7-fold difference between our most and least successful tests
  • Video might be a valuable way to fundraise and “sell” education in the future; we are testing this now
  • TdF is very close to raising $1 million by the end of 2016
  • TCOYD proposed doing a 50/50 split of funds for the Diabetes Forum, and will give TdF the first 30,000
 
Advocacy
  • TdF sent letter to FDA leadership, which was signed by 22 other diabetes organizations
  • The team is writing a manuscript on the survey results presented at the FDA to be published in Clinical Diabetes; considering an abstract for ADA
  • Starting to plan a consensus conference on outcomes beyond A1c to change FDA guidance
  • Sent recommendations on CVD to the Chan-Zuckerberg initiative through Sheryl Sandberg
  • TdF won two panels at SXSW 2017, and one will feature Dr. Susan Cox from the Dell Medical School
  • Anthology program has taken off, with 50 completed interviews with thought leaders within the diabetes and public health fields
  • The Anthology will eventually include additional sections (Ruth recommended sticking to 3 sections)
  • Kelly has been more selective about speaking engagements, and leveraging them for networking
  • The team has drafted a guide on Best Practices for Manufacturers to Engage People with Diabetes, and is looking for options on where to publish
 
Connecting
  • d17 will be September 27-29, 2017; Dennis has generously agreed to host the first afternoon at the IDEO office in San Francisco
  • Considering options for patient guests to invite and lead small group discussions
