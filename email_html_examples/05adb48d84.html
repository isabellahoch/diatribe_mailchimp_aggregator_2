In our upcoming issue of diaTribe, we’ve written all about the incredible FDA approvals of Medtronic’s MiniMed 670G and Abbott’s FreeStyle Libre Pro – along with much, much more. We’ve put together an in-depth article, complete with FAQs on the FDA approval of Medtronic’s MiniMed 670G Hybrid Closed Loop System – the very first automated basal insulin delivery system, available across the US starting in Spring 2017. In this comprehensive piece, we’ve included the answers to some of the most pressing questions related to the 670G, like: what really is the MiniMed 670G? Is there evidence that it works? How much will it cost? How can someone get the 670G if they’re already on a Medtronic pump, and how can someone get it if they’re not? How safe is it? Along with all of this critical information, our feature also discusses what’s next in store from Medtronic – including details on the launch of the MiniMed Pro Infusion with BD FlowSmart technology (Adam and I just tested this yesterday - he likes it! Let us know if you'd like more of his thoughts!) He also has just demo'd the Sugar.IQ app and you'll see more about that too. By the way, I just got off the phone with Dr. Aaron Kowalski from the JDRF – he’s already on the new infusion set and he LOVES it .... 

We’re so excited to feature a piece on ANOTHER ground-breaking approval: the long-awaited FDA approval of the FreeStyle Libre Pro Professional CGM System, along with the phenomenal announcement from Abbott that its “consumer” real-time version of FreeStyle Libre has been submitted to the FDA for review. After more than a year in review, the FreeStyle Libre Pro will be available in US physician’s offices in the coming weeks, allowing doctors and nurses to collect much better, broader, more comprehensive data on their patients’ glucose levels, and, in turn, better personalize their treatments (that’s ideally therapy and also diets and exercise and maybe even ways of addressing stress). In this article, we discuss the Libre Pro’s advantages, costs, and accessibility, as well as the differences between the Libre Pro and the “consumer version”, which may be approved by the FDA in early 2017 (there are already over 125,000 people in Europe who are using it).
