d18: The Third Executive Innovation Lab
on Diabetes and Pre-Diabetes
An Initiative of The diaTribe Foundation 
Only a few months ago, The diaTribe Foundation hosted “d17: Executive Innovation Lab on Diabetes and Prediabetes” a highly selective, three-day gathering of brilliant medical, scientific, policy, and education leaders from healthcare and technology. Participants worked on changing the conversation and recommending actions regarding type 2 diabetes and pre-diabetes by applying the concepts of design thinking and behavior design models. To learn more about this gathering, as well as our inaugural d16 Lab, click here to access the d17 summary report and click here to read the d16 Consensus of Ideas.
In 2018, we will continue the conversation with an intimate gathering designed to drive greater innovation, collaboration, and impact in diabetes and prediabetes. And we’d like you to join us. This year, we plan to move into action. We will:
  • ENVISION and EXPAND initiatives that address the most pressing challenges in type 2 diabetes using novel frameworks like behavior design and behavior design; 
  • DIVE into prevention, focusing on tools and models that support the health of our communities and our nation;
  • ARTICULATE key obstacles preventing progress and identify the most pressing and addressable problems in the field; 
  • PINPOINT bright spots and gaps in diabetes and ask: what is currently successful, what needs improvement, and what is most scalable?
  • BUILD on the highly innovative, systems-levels solutions conceptualized by d16 and d17 participants, all of which can reduce the societal burden of diabetes and heighten the urgency for action around the epidemic; and 
  • EXPLORE how cross-sector collaboration can help us develop integrated, systems-level approaches to diabetes prevention and behavior change; and 
  • CELEBRATE successes in the field, including the launch of the Anthology of Bright Spots, a unified collection of successful programs that have improved outcomes related to diabetes.
