In the latest Adam’s Corner, Adam takes on one of his biggest “diabetes landmines” – the hypoglycemia binge. While it’s easy to over-eat to correct a low and then induce a chain of subsequent highs and lows, Adam explains his plan of attack to avoid stumbling. Read here to learn more about his automatic treatment plan and tips that help him stay on track. These are just a few of the many invaluable strategies for defeating diabetes landmines found in Bright Spots & Landmines: The Diabetes Guide I wish Someone Handed Me.
 
The diaTribe team recently sat down with renowned author Gary Taubes to discuss his latest book, The Case Against Sugar, and the role of carbohydrates in obesity. In this diaTribe Dialogue, Taubes discusses whether sugar is actually an addictive substance, why he thinks “the nature of a healthy diet should not be a matter of opinion but of fact,” and the need for more randomized trials studying nutrition. More here.
 
Exciting news on the regulatory front – the FDA recently approved Tandem’s t:slim X2 pump with integrated Dexcom G5 CGM, allowing t:slim X2 users to upgrade pump software at home for free using the Tandem Device Updater. Though t:slim X2/G5 does not yet include automated insulin delivery algorithms, CGM/pump integration is an important step as Tandem beings studies of its predictive low glucose suspend (PLGS) system with Dexcom G5.
 
Finally, this article is among the very biggest news we can remember in years. The FDA just announced an updated product label for Victoza, a once-daily injectable GLP-1 agonist for people with type 2 diabetes. Victoza now, according to the FDA, significantly lowers the risk of heart attacks and strokes (and death!) on top of other approved indications including lowering A1c and helping manage blood sugars. Wow! If you are at risk for heart disease and have type 2 diabetes, you can now take a medicine to reduce your risk! While Victoza also helps lower blood sugar, we think it’s amazing that a therapy does this - wow! This is similar to Jardiance. Does this work for reducing CVD in type 1? We don't know, since it's not even approved for type 1, but the pressure will be back on to engage.
