In addition to an afternoon at IDEO, a day in Union Square, and optional final morning at the Ferry Building filled with innovation, collaboration, and discussion, we also have some exciting events and dinners planned which we hope will help immerse you in this beautiful city while you are here. As a special welcome on September 27, we have reserved tickets at Pier 24 – a hidden, quiet gallery space located right next door to the IDEO office on the waterfront. It was established by a famous investor in San Francisco, Andy Pilara, in 2010 after being inspired by SFMOMA's Diane Arbus exhibit several years earlier. The collection now has over 4,000 photographs. It's been one of my favorite spots in the city since it was built, and is still a hidden gem, open by appointment only to very small groups. The current exhibition, called The Grain of the Present, features the breathtaking work of ten contemporary photographers who seek to examine everyday life in a new way. We hope that you may find inspiration in these photographs and similar motivation to examine and discuss the most urgent public health of our time in a new way during the d17 Executive Innovation Lab.

As the website notes, Pier 24 Photography houses the permanent collection of the Pilara Foundation, which is dedicated to collecting, preserving and exhibiting photography. The foundation seeks to engage the community through exhibitions, publications, and public programs, and it welcomes members of the public, academic institutions, and museum groups by appointment so if you can't attend the 1 pm tour (which will be guided), remember for next time (you can reserve a spot up to 30 days in advance). The history of the building is fascinating - the Pilara Foundation was the first to inhabit this historic space in nearly three decades. After two years spent remodeling the building, Pier 24 Photography opened to the public in 2010: "The scale of the space allows for the Foundation’s collection to be displayed with few restrictions, and the location off the Embarcadero promenade, directly underneath the Bay Bridge, provides for a breathtaking panoramic view of the Bay. Pier 24 respects the original use and intention of the building, pays homage to a remarkable collection of photography, and highlights the Bay Area’s stunning landscape."
 
We have tickets reserved for the 1:00 pm and 10:00 am slots on Wednesday, September 27. We will kick start our official program at IDEO, right next door to Pier 24, at 3:00 pm. Please email us to let us know if you would like one or two of these tickets, and for which time (feel free to bring someone not attending d17). Section Three of The Anthology of Bright Spots in Type 2 Diabetes and Prediabetes – Health Care Teams of the Future: this section features creative programs that take new approaches to health care through teams that value collaboration, innovation, and problem-solving. We feel we've been hearing for decades about how healthcare teams "could" or "should" be transformed and we wanted to take a look at this, particularly given how obvious it's become that the 1:1 team of the past (doctor:patient) will not scale.
