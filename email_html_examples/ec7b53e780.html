It’s so easy (and unfortunately so common!) to view blood sugar numbers as “good” or “bad” “grades” on diabetes performance. This type of relationship with diabetes data can drive a lot of self-blame and guilt, Adam says. In his latest Adam’s Corner, Adam shares a simple mindset trick from Bright Spots & Landmines that radically transformed how he views his blood glucose data. Read more to learn how Adam reframed BG numbers into neutral data points that help him take action, not judgment. I've really tried to take to heart Adam's thoughts on this and I have found feeling less guilty can enable more room in my head to enable better health. While I don't always remember it, when I do, I have felt quite liberated - this has been very helpful for me and I hope it is for you too if you get caught in this guilt trap thingy.

Speaking of mindset and diabetes, a recent report from the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) found that 1 in 7 adolescents (12-17 years old) with type 1 or type 2 diabetes had a major depressive episode in the past year, compared to 1 in 11 adolescents without diabetes. Our team talked with experts to learn how guardians can be the most supportive of adolescents and teens with diabetes – read their recommendations here.
 
Is your blood glucose meter accurate? New results from the Diabetes Technology Society’s (DTS) study of 18 popular blood glucose meters used in the U.S. showed that only six of the 7 devices met the passing standard for meter accuracy. Find out which brands passed and which didn’t make the cut in our article.
 
Finally, it’s great to see that Medicare coverage of Dexcom’s G5 CGM is moving forward, despite some early delays. The first “test case” Medicare G5 CGM bundles were shipped in late July to a subset of users; if these are successfully reimbursed by Medicare, Dexcom will ship more in the latter half of 2017. More here.
