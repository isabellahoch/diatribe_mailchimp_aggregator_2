December 31, 2016
 

Dear Expert Panelists for the diaTribe Foundation's Anthology of Bright Spots,

We are excited to wish you a happy new year and to share with you, as promised, The Anthology of Bright Spots section on type 2 diabetes prevention. We are so grateful for the feedback and guidance you have already given us, which we have done our best to incorporate into our work, and we look forward to your thoughts. The link here is to a word document for you to download.
 
In the two months since we first started compiling a number of programs to share with you, we have conducted over 50 interviews (many of them with the brilliant people that you suggested!), screened and researched an additional 40 prevention efforts, and had informal conversations on dozens more. We attended the 9th World Congress on the Prevention of Diabetes and its Complications in Atlanta (here is a report on this meeting written by associates at our sister organization, Close Concerns), where we met dozens of experts and learned from three full days of packed sessions. And, although what you are receiving today comes in an admittedly dense word document, we have also started assembling the team of designers who will put together a fabulous presentation of the Anthology once the content is finalized for this and future parts. And, that's where you come in - getting to the final product! 
 
We now share with you the draft result of our efforts: The Anthology of Bright Spots in the prevention of type 2 diabetes. 
While we are proud of the Anthology, our work is not complete. As we begin 2017, we will interview experts with whom we were unable to schedule times in November and December. We will be working with our design and web development partners to create a navigable online version so that it can be more easily accessed, disseminated, and used. (If you are interested in seeing examples of its work, the design team helped us create d16 materials including State of the Field and Consensus of Ideas, and we are hoping to work with the same web developer with whom we have partnered for our website at The diaTribe Foundation and diaTribe.org.) Finally, in tandem with all of these efforts, we will be considering and then finalizing what the topics of future sections beyond prevention will be - most likely, there will be three in all. We welcome advice or suggestions from you on how we might best approach any of these forthcoming pieces of our work - our board has been discussing this and if you are interested in the topics, please tell us!
 
Even while we acknowledge that this work is ongoing, it is critical to seek your feedback at this point. You are the first to see our draft of the Anthology in its fully assembled form, and your suggestions, as always, will guide its direction and improvement. We acknowledge at the end of our draft all the tremendous help we have had in building this first part of the Anthology; more than anyone, we owe so much to Dr. James Gavin who provided the first inspiration for this piece and has given us incredible guidance. Here's to your reading, and we look forward to working with you in 2017 to determine what the easiest and most fruitful and productive way there will be to capture your feedback on the anthology.
Here is some fine print on how the Anthology is presented. After a brief introduction to the project, its goals, and our methods, the first major section focuses on the 25 prevention programs themselves that have been selected to answer the question "what's working? what's scalable?". As the introduction explains, we have divided the programs into three categories – “Gold Standards,” “Prime Performers,” and “Innovators.” The meaning of each is explained in the Anthology’s introduction. Our decision to use separate categories was inspired by your valuable feedback to our initial sample of summaries; our aim is clarify the different aspects of a program that can make it “shine” as a Bright Spot. We'd love your feedback on whether you think this works. 
 
The second section is called “Insights.” We found, in our research, that the conversations themselves yielded critical learning that should be culled and highlighted. Our interviewees, including all of you, have spoken powerfully about prevention from many angles, identifying both the challenges and opportunities, and frankly inspiring us to do even more.  Thus was born our Insights, which bring together some of the most valuable lessons we learned. 
 
The final section is an Appendix including names and titles and organizations of over 50-plus people we interviewed and summaries of select interviews. We are in the process of distilling a number of the conversations that we’ve had as recently as earlier this week and will be adding to this in January. There are also names of additional people we would like to interview (in process) and we would love your suggestions on others who are very smart about prevention that are missing. Although the Anthology currently has a largely American focus, we ultimately surely aim, as discussed with many of you, to have examples from multiple continents and will be seeking help on that aspect of the Anthology in particular. Please let us know if you have ideas here as well! 
 
To be clear, we are not expecting you to serve as editors for this project. We are grateful for your willingness to support us in this project, and we obviously do not want it to be an undue burden on your time. While we value feedback in any form, we most hope for your big-picture advice as well as your specific advice: How can we strengthen this project as a whole and how can we sharpen its presentation? What might we still be missing? Very importantly, what should not be included?  What will be the best way(s) to strengthen the prevention program section as well as the insights section? As our Expert Panelists for prevention, we also deeply value your input on how this project will make the greatest impact and we will begin addressing that after we finish work on the content - but we are thinking about it already and value your perspectives immensely.
  
Thank you for your valued contributions. We are assuming for now that phone calls with each of you may be the best way to get your broad feedback; if you are willing, we would also love your comments on the Anthology as it stands now using track changes. If you are open to this, please turn on track changes before making edits and comments. We want to emphasize, one more time, that although this is a fairly dense document, this is not how we would intend ultimately to distribute it - it is one "big" document but if you prefer to give feedback on one section only, that is fine! Of course, please do not hesitate to contact us with any questions. We will be back in touch in one to two weeks to discuss how you'd best like to give feedback on this project. If you do not want to send a document back but wish to speak by phone, we can start those phone calls as soon as you would like.

All our immense thanks to you on this work on diabetes prevention as you ring in the new year! We so appreciate, enormously, you taking part in this initiative with us and we promise to make it worth your while. 
 
Very Best,
Ben Pallant, Amelia Dmowska, Kelly Close, and The diaTribe Team
