4. PRE-READING: BEHAVIOR DESIGN
We are thrilled that celebrated behavior scientist and innovator Dr. BJ Fogg, PhD will be leading a workshop on behavior design at d17. Dr. Fogg leads the Persuasive Technology Lab at Stanford University and has created countless new models and methods for changing behavior. He is the author of Persuasive Technology: Using Computers to Change What We Think and Do. Fortune Magazine even selected Fogg as one of 10 “New Gurus You Should Know” back in 2008 - boy were they right. All to say, we can’t wait to learn and discuss behavior change with Dr. Fogg!
 
In 30 minutes, you can prepare for Dr. Fogg’s workshop by reading the following:
  • Top 10 Mistakes in Behavior Change (10 minutes)
  • Behavior Model Website (10 minutes)
  • “How I Cracked the Code for Creating Habits” (10 minutes)
If you have time, we also highly recommend Dr. Fogg’s informative and engaging TED talk on Tiny Habits - it's only 17 minutes and has nearly 1 million views. In the past five years Dr. Fogg has coached over 42,000 people in this behavior change method.

8. BREAKOUT GROUP QUESTIONS
Finally, we’re excited to share the breakout group questions we’ll be discussing during the afternoon portion of our innovation lab (see below)! If you’re itching to serve as a “breakout group leader” on one of these topics, please let us know and we’ll fill you in on the details : >.
  • How might we create a movement to reduce stigma and improve public attitudes and social norms surrounding diabetes and prediabetes?
  • How might we change and/or create policy that aggressively promotes health and wellness?
  • How might we design programs that are best-suited to cause sustainable behavior change?
  • How might we re-design the acute-care-oriented healthcare system to better serve chronic disease management?
  • How might we make diabetes and pre-diabetes prevention a larger priority in the health system and beyond?
  • How might reducing the burden of type 2 diabetes and prediabetes become more attractive to funders and philanthropists?
  • How might programs showcased in the Anthology of Bright Spots (prevention, workplace wellness, and healthcare team of the future) be scaled?
