1. Our landmark consensus meeting, “Glycemic Outcomes Beyond A1c,” held in Bethesda, MD on July 21, was phenomenally successful and productive. The room was packed with over 140 attendees – including 23 honored diabetes leaders who presented, debated, and facilitated break-out groups – as well as 120 participants watching via live-stream. The gathering aimed to highlight consensus on key glycemic metrics, with the overarching goal to demonstrate to the FDA that A1c is necessary but no longer sufficient as a measure of diabetes management. To do so, leaders discussed the importance of measuring and monitoring glycemic outcomes that matter to patients and providers, such as hypoglycemia, time in range, and glycemic variability. Attendees also engaged in conversations surrounding terminology, translation, education, and implementation challenges related to the Beyond A1c Movement. We have compiled a comprehensive resource page for the gathering, which includes the diaTribe.org coverage, the agenda, the video compilation of patient and provider voices shown at the meeting, presentations from speakers, background information and history, a link to watch the video of the entire conference, and some pictures of the incredible interactions that occurred. In addition, if you have not yet had a chance to read Close Concerns’ coverage of the day, check it out here.

In this crucial period of momentum, we have assembled a writing committee (list included below) to provide guidance in generating a consensus report, which we hope to submit to Diabetes Care by early October. We will focus on detailing the proceedings of the meeting, the points of consensus achieved (e.g., glucose metrics), and the outstanding questions that will guide next steps necessary to advance the Beyond A1c Movement. On the internal team side, we have distributed a survey to all attendees to gather feedback on their experience during the meeting. We will compile this feedback, along with learnings from debrief conversations among our team, to create two reports: a specific consensus debrief document and a more general “how to” guide that will detail steps and considerations in planning advocacy events for future use.

2. d17 Executive Innovation Lab in Diabetes and Prediabetes: We are thrilled that behavior design expert Dr. BJ Fogg, PhD, will be joining d17 as a facilitator and participant. He will lead a two-hour session on behavior change that is specifically tailored to focus on diabetes and prediabetes. To read more about his work, please visit http://www.bjfogg.com/. We are currently combing through our d17 agenda with Heather to assure that we design a convening full of innovation and collaboration. 3. The Anthology of Bright Spots is being prepared for launch at d17. It features 55 programs and is based on the input of 100 brilliant thinkers and advocates. The three sections, focused on Prevention, Workplace Wellness, and Healthcare Teams of the Future, are being sent to d17 participants in advance of the event, and the searchable online version, which will be housed in The diaTribe Foundation’s website, is being finalized for launch at d17.

EASD – diaTribe’s “Solvable Problems in Diabetes” Event: We are gearing up for our fourth annual fundraising event, “Solvable Problems in Diabetes,” during the European Association for the Study of Diabetes’ (EASD) annual meeting in Lisbon, Portugal. The event will be held the evening of Tuesday, September 12th from 7-9 p.m. at the Pavilhão de Portugal, an architectural marvel situated at the mouth of the Tagus river and steps away from the conference venue. We are excited to present an evening of discussion on the most urgent problems and opportunities facing diabetes with a panel of eminent leaders: Dr. Daniel Drucker of the University of Toronto, Dr. Melanie Davies of the University of Leicester, and Dr. David Matthews of the University of Oxford. We look forward to hearing the insights of these key opinion leaders and raising funds for our foundation’s programs. We have already secured funding for this event from Abbott, BI, Fenix, Insulet, Profil, Prosciento, and Roche, and are actively pursuing other sponsors including Novo Nordisk, Sanofi, Lilly, Medtronic, Merck, Bigfoot, and Gilead since we are still very behind fundraising from EASD 2016! 

5. diaTribe.org: With extra hands on deck this summer, we’ve published multiple diaTribe issues since the last board meeting, several of them containing A/B tests as well! Most recently, we are testing one long mailer against two, more frequent short mailers.  

Year-to-date, we’ve had about one million site users totaling nearly 1.5 million unique diaTribe.org page views. As for subscribers, we have had approximately 15k new subscribers year-to-date, with ~115k current subscribers. This number will grow substantially when we add the subscribers that slipped through the cracks (as you recall, one of our sign-up forms hadn’t been pulling the subscribers through to the mailing list, but we have fixed the issue and identified the missing subscribers!) as well as those captured through our partnership with Diabetes Daily, amounting to almost 17k new subscribers. Still, we are also short on our year-end goal of 140k, which we will look forward to discussing with you.
 
Content-wise, our best performing pieces remain the “10 Diet Commandments,” “22 Factors that Influence Blood Glucose,” and the Abbott FreeStyle Libre Test Drive. The “10 Diet Commandments” and “22 Factors that Influence Blood Glucose” account for about half of the new subscribers obtained since the last board meeting in June. As always, we are working with Media Cause to experiment with strategies to more efficiently meet our goals. One new thing we tried in July was a Facebook giveaway of Adam’s book, Bright Spots & Landmines, to capture emails. Not only were we able to capture more than 700 emails, but we were also able to drive downloads of Adam’s book (see below for more on that!).

This information reinforces the knowledge that Adam’s Corner pieces on actionable tips do extremely well with our audience, as do big tech updates, particularly on FreeStyle Libre. Our bottom three from the most recent mailer provide evidence that Quotable Quotes articles from events are not read widely, nor are industry partnership updates and Trial Watches. As we continue to collect information on what works and what doesn’t, we would love any ideas or feedback on what we should be writing about more, what we should be writing less of, and how to better market the things we want to be read! For instance, our pieces in the Lilly-sponsored Access series about topics such as navigating the appeals process and employers and insurance have had some readership but not a huge amount of traffic. Additionally, we are constantly looking to bolster our “evergreen” resource pages. We are always looking for ways to expand and improve diaTribe, and we welcome any and all feedback!
 
6. Bright Spots & Landmines is now in the hands of over 15,000 people! More than 10,000 have been direct purchases or downloads, and, most excitingly, the number of downloads and purchases increased from June to July and remains on track to increase further in August. In addition, diaTribe has given away nearly 5,000 copies at conferences and through purchases by diabetes corporations, most recently including 800 copies being claimed in an hour at the AADE annual meeting in Indianapolis. Bright Spots & Landmines has brought over 3,000 new subscribers to diaTribe, contributed to more than 50k page views on diaTribe.org, and has generated more than 2 million social media impressions through the hashtag #MyBrightSpots.
