Following his recent article on “42 Factors That Affect Blood Sugar?! A Surprising Update,” Adam reflects on his strategies to conquer these 42 factors in his latest Adam’s Corner. For instance, not all 42 of these factors are created equal; instead, carbohydrate quantity and type have a significant impact on blood sugar spikes, and glucose monitoring is the best way to learn what works. In this piece, he also recommends focusing on factors that have “ripple effects,” adding consistency to his diabetes routine, taking an “If This, Then That” approach, and keeping in mind that learning and improvement are continuous. More here.
 
In our latest New Now Next, we report on the MiniMed Mio Advance – a new pump infusion set inserter that features an all-in-one, fully disposable inserter with a fully hidden needle. This inserter is compatible with Medtronic pumpers and those with a luer-lock connector (Roche, Animas). The inserter is currently available in Europe, and we hope to see the US on the list of countries launching later this year. To learn more about this new innovative pump infusion sets, click here.
 
Finally, our team traveled to Houston to attend the graduation of the first class of the “Congregational Health Leadership” training program, part of Cities Changing Diabetes’ innovative Faith and Diabetes Initiative. This program trains lay members from various religious communities to lead their own diabetes education program in their houses of worship, leveraging the existing infrastructure of faith communities to spread messages of diabetes prevention, health, and wellness. Read our full coverage for more details about the reach and scope of this creative program.
