I am thrilled to share a sneak preview of our upcoming issue of diaTribe!

We kick off our coverage with exciting advances in treating and detecting diabetic retinopathy. Biotech giant Genentech recently announced FDA approval of Lucentis as the first and only medication to treat all forms of diabetic retinopathy. An announcement from IBM Watson followed, with results from an impressive study showing that IBM’s Watson technology is learning how to diagnose diabetic retinopathy. More on these developments here.
 
Secondly, the FDA recently cleared Sanofi’s “My Dose Coach App” for adjusting basal insulin doses. The app – which is prescribed and set up by a health care provider – uses fasting blood glucose and hypoglycemia data to suggest optimized basal insulin injection doses.
 
We also report on results from the new DELIVER 2 study which indicate that people with type 2 diabetes who switched to Sanofi’s “next generation” basal insulin, Tuojeo, experienced reduced risk of hypoglycemia. This important real world study complements similar findings from the earlier SWITCH 2 study, which also found that a next-generation insulin (Novo Nordisk’s Tresiba) decreased hypoglycemia compared to “first generation” Lantus.
 
In other hypoglycemia news, the JDRF and Helmsley Charitable Trust just hosted a pivotal workshop, “How Do You Prevent Hypoglycemia in Type 1 Diabetes?” Leading thinkers gathered to review what’s known about hypoglycemia, what questions still need to be answered, and what’s next – including the possibility of a drug-based therapy for hypoglycemia.
 
Finally, the release date for Bright Spots and Landmines: The Diabetes Guide I Wish Someone Had Handed Me is officially set for May 9! In his book, Adam shares some of his favorite recipes for homerun meals with diabetes, including his recipe for “The Three Minute Diabetes Breakfast That Changes Lives” – aka chia seed pudding!
