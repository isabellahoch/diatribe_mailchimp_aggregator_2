CONSENSUS OF IDEAS
From the hundreds of ideas from d17, we asked for your input on which ones would have the most impact on improving the lives of people with diabetes and prediabetes. Based on your feedback, we identified the top 12 ideas, and their working titles are the following:   
 
1. Scaling & Spreading the “Healthy Beverage Initiative Toolkit” to Reduce Consumption of Sugar-Sweetened Beverages
2. Designing Interventions for Pregnant Women   
3. Food as Medicine: Expanding Healthy Food Prescription Programs Nationwide (see our recent piece on Geisinger and you know how enthused we are about Dr. Rita Nguyen, UCSF ...) 
4. Continuous Glucose Monitoring as a Public Awareness Program for Healthcare Professionals
5. Reaching Consensus on What “Healthy” Really Means
6. A “Diabetes Friendly” Symbol for Food, Restaurants, and More
7. “300 Small Healthy Behaviors”—A Toolkit for HCPs (see below on related front)
8. Integrating Cues for Healthy Behavior Change into Existing Technology
9. Behavior Design x Public Health Training
10. Creating Healthy Buildings by Adapting LEED Standards
11. Building momentum around Show Up Day
12. Addressing Chronic Disease Management & Prevention by Creating High-Quality Resources for Healthcare Providers (this stems from broader work stemming from d16 on education for HCPs - diaTribe recently received a $250,000 grant from the Helmsley Charitable Trust for further work on this)
