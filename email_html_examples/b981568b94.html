In this sneak preview of our upcoming issue of diaTribe, we’re excited to share an in-depth piece with everything you need to know about Patient Assistance Programs (PAPs), a new Trial Watch testing a device to help prevent cardiac arrest in people with diabetes, an announcement regarding Insulet’s Omnipod Dash, and big news for The diaTribe Foundation – a new grant to explore creating a new diaTribe publication specifically for healthcare professionals.  
 
In our last article in the diaTribe Series on Access, we provide a comprehensive list of Patient Assistance Programs (PAPs) which help people who meet certain eligibility requirements get diabetes drugs for free. Our article is based on six-month-long research into these programs, and breaks down current PAPs in the US by manufacturer to help people find the program right for them. Plus, we give an overview of what a PAP application looks like, what the requirements are, and tips to complete the application with healthcare providers. Read about other topics related to access, like insurance appeals, employee rights at work, making the switch to Medicare with diabetes, and saving money on diabetes care here.
 
In a new Trial Watch, we share information about a trial to test a subcutaneous implantable cardioverter defibrillator (S-ICD), a device that can help people with diabetes who have had a heart attack with relatively preserved heart function. This device works to prevent cardiac arrests and treat life-threatening, abnormal heart rhythms.To read about who qualifies, trial locations, and other inclusion criteria, click here.  
 
If you haven’t yet heard – Insulet recently announced that it submitted its Omnipod Dash touchscreen handheld Personal Diabetes Manager (PDM) to the FDA, just a few weeks after news of forthcoming Medicare coverage for Omnipod. If approved, the Dash PDM will allow Omnipod users control their insulin pump through Bluetooth connection, and improves user experience with smartphone viewing and remote monitoring. Read more about the device’s new features, including a “view” app for caregivers, here. 
 
Finally, we’re excited to share big news for The diaTribe Foundation! We are thrilled that we have recently received a new grant from the Helmsley Charitable Trust to explore the possibility of a creating a new diabetes-focused resource for healthcare professionals, potentially called “diaTribe for HCPs.” We are so grateful to Helmsley for this opportunity to explore the expansion of our work and empower healthcare providers to improve patient care. To learn more, read our blog post or the full announcement from Helmsley and The diaTribe Foundation.  
 
We hope you enjoyed this full preview of diaTribe! Thank you for your support of our labor of love.
