January 2017
 
Dear CPS Lectures friend,
 
We are so excited to announce our CPS Lectures line-up for 2017! John and some wonderful friends began this series for me in 2003 when I was, with my brother Patrick and our mom, in the midst of tremendous grief following my father's death. This series began because our dad loved to learn as much as possible from friends around us. CPS Lectures has evolved into a community discussion series that we never could have imagined, especially now that we are approaching 110 gatherings over nearly 15 years. Today, we wanted to thank you for helping create this series in which so many of us have learned and shared so tremendously. It has been a major gift to us, and we begin the series in 2017 with insightful and stimulating speakers and discussion to look forward to ... we'll be adding to our list all year and here are some dates to save and advance notices! 

Save the Date (Winter/Spring 2017):
  • Thursday, January 26, 2017 - TONIGHT!: Stephen Satterfield - Food Writing Fellow for Civil Eats, former General Manger of one of America’s best restaurants, Nopa, and founder of the popular digital media food channel Nopalize. RSVP here - we SO hope to see you this evening! 
  • Wednesday, March 1, 2017: Lisa Kay Solomon - Thought leader on design innovation and author of Moments of Impact: How to Design Strategic Conversations That Accelerate Change and Design a Better Business: New Tools, Skills and Mindset for Strategy and Innovation. RSVP here.

Eventbrite with RSVP coming soon. 
  • Tuesday, April 4, 2017: Bruce Feiler - New York Times best-selling author of Walking the Bible, Abraham, and The Council of Dads, and, most recently, The First Love Story: Adam, Eve, and Us, as well as the author of the popular “This Life” column for the Sunday New York Times. (Due to Bruce's schedule, this is our first lunchtime lecture! We know many cannot break away at lunch, and our apologies in advance! Let us know if you'd like his book!) RSVP here. 
  • Wednesday, April 12, 2017: Emily Esfahani Smith – Author of The Power of Meaning: Crafting a Life that Matters and expert in positive psychology. Emily's writing has appeared in publications like the Wall Street Journal, New York Times, The Atlantic, and others. RSVP here.

For the New Year, we also have a brand new Facebook page and Instagram (@cpslectures). Follow us to stay up to date with the latest speakers and upcoming events and if you know anyone who would like to help build these social media channels (as part of the community manager for CPS Lectures or not!), please let us know!

As we kick off 2017, we are reminded of your support of our lecture series to date and we're so grateful. We love that this series has been free since its inception and your gifts have been very generous and have helped enormously in our subsidy of the cost of drinks and food, occasional air tickets for speakers, and low-key management of the event.

In terms of related progress, many of you know that CPS Lectures is a program of our 501c3, The diaTribe Foundation. We are thrilled to say that we exceeded our 2016 year-end fundraising goal of $1.0 million that goes toward tremendous education for our 100,000-plus subscribers to diaTribe.org and advocacy for the 20 million people with diabetes in the US alone ~ as we start our fourth full year as a 501c3, we send giant appreciation to so many of you that helped us reach this new mark as we focus on our work to help people with diabetes and pre-diabetes. 
 
And, as we begin the new year, it's not too late to make a difference!
 
Our 2017 goal is ambitious, even audacious, and we appreciate your help at every moment throughout the year as we look to grow The diaTribe Foundation and our patient resource diaTribe. Even $5 would make a difference! And, if you have ideas for small or large grants (nothing is too big!) we might be able to get as a community series or for our nonprofit, please let us know.
