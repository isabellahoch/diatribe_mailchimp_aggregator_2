At ADA, we were on the edges of our seats with the rest of the conference goers to learn the results from two blockbuster trials – DEVOTE and CANVAS – examining the impact of Tresiba and Invokana, respectively, on heart health. On a very exciting note, DEVOTE showed that basal insulin Tresiba (see background on Tresiba here) reduces the risk of severe hypoglycemia by 40% (and by a whopping 53% overnight) versus Lantus, in addition to being safe for the heart. The CANVAS trial had mixed results, demonstrating that Invokana has positive cardioprotective and renal outcomes (yay!), but also, based on a stricter way of collecting data, showing an increase in risk for lower-limb amputations by two-fold. At this stage, there isn’t much agreement among thought leaders, and we are unclear exactly how to react to these results. Along with the experts, we’re looking forward to seeing results from larger databases. For now, our main advice for those on the drugs is to talk to their healthcare team and remember that their teams may also still be digesting the results.
 
On a bright note, Adam has written an amazing column on the importance of getting a good night’s rest and the impact of sleep on blood sugar! One of the most underrated diabetes management tools is sleep, says Adam, and this article provides so many smart, doable, actionable tips for getting a better night’s sleep. Adam’s advice on sleep can also be found in one of the many insightful chapters in Bright Spots and Landmines. Please let us know if you don’t already have a copy of his book, and we’ll send you one right away! 
 
Big news! An FDA advisory panel voted 17-2 this past Tuesday to update Victoza’s label to reflect a reduction in heart risk, as shown in the LEADER trial. We’re excited to share this promising development, and we look forward to hearing an official decision from the FDA later this year.
 
And that’s not it! The wonderful Kerri Sparling is back at it – after taking some time off from diaTribe while she had her second little baby, she’s back writing again for diaTribe.
