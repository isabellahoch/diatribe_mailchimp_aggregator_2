To kick off the year, I've just written a piece in the Huffington Post with my reflections on the progress in diabetes in 2016, and why we absolutely cannot lose momentum in 2017. In this piece, I discuss some of the past year's biggest milestones in patient advocacy, innovative new treatments, and the latest advances in tech. Now more than ever, I think it is critical to build off of the past year's progress so that we can work to enhance the quality of diabetes care and increase access to that care.

Read on for your first sneak peak of diaTribe this year, featuring articles on the closed loop/automated insulin-front, tips for staying healthy and meeting your 2017 resolutions, and much more…
 
2016 saw some of the most critical milestones, progress, and changes related to a closed loop/automated insulin delivery system. “2016: A Pivotal Year for Closed Loop/Automated Insulin Delivery,” penned by  Adam Brown and an outstanding team, covers everything you need to know about the biggest achievements of this year – including the players, products, and launch timing of systems – PLUS an in-depth FAQ section that answers questions like, “What do these systems cost?,” “Which option is best for me?,” and “Are these systems truly ‘automated’?” We share updates on the latest devices from Medtronic, Tandem, Bigfoot, Beta Bionics, Animas, and Insulet; we also have an overview of do-it-yourself (DIY) automated insulin delivery systems like OpenAPS, Loop, and AndroidAPS.
 
2016 also closed with a groundbreaking announcement – on December 20, the FDA approved Dexcom’s G5 continuous glucose monitor (CGM) for making diabetes treatment decisions without the need for confirmatory fingersticks. In our article on the announcement, we discuss the implications of this “official” FDA approval – including encouraging the use of CGM, increasing safety for current CGM users, and paving the path for Medicare coverage. It’s particularly gratifying to see this approval so soon after The diaTribe Foundation sent a letter to the FDA that received nearly 10,000 signatures in support of using CGMs for insulin dosing; see the original diaTribe article for more – FDA Panel Votes YES for Using Dexcom’s G5 CGM for Insulin Dosing.

Finally, we hope you’re making progress on your health goals for this year. Our team had the opportunity to sit down with Dr. Shreela Sharma, an expert in realistic goal setting and co-founder of the amazing program Brighter Bites to discuss overcoming the biggest barriers to meeting health resolutions. She shared her top seven tips for effective healthy behavior change, like setting one goal at a time, building health into existing habits, and surrounding yourself with positive influences. What are your health goals related to diabetes for 2017 and how does Dr. Sharma's advice relate? We’d love to hear!

We couldn’t be more excited as we look ahead to all the possibilities that these next 350-something days have to offer.  Hopefully they will be filled with progress for people with diabetes worldwide. Thank you for your continuous support and all you do to help us in our labor of love.
