On January 12, a most memorable day for so many people with diabetes, the US Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) announced the first key step toward Medicare coverage of CGMs. This ruling laid the path to Medicare reimbursement for CGMs with an insulin-dosing claim, like Dexcom’s G5 CGM (currently the only device in the US with this indication). The news came months earlier than expected, which gives us hope that reimbursements will come soon for those on Medicare. Patients and advocates have been working hard for this milestone; The diaTribe Foundation was active with the JDRF and other diabetes advocacy organizations at a June 2016 FDA Advisory Committee Meeting for Dexcom’s G5 (watch clips from the Open Public Hearing and panel vote here) and we were proud to have the Joslin's Dr. Lori Laffel and multiple others representing The diaTribe Foundation through eloquent speeches. What’s more, over 10,000 people with diabetes and their families signed a letter to the FDA written by diaTribe supporting use of Dexcom CGM for insulin dosing. We are incredibly thankful to the scientists, researchers, health care practitioners, people with diabetes and their families for their work in paving the way for coverage of this vital technology. Now, there are still lots of details to be worked out - we'll be in touch along the way as more becomes clear. 
 
Our second feature in this issue is coverage of Lilly’s new partnership with Express Scripts, offering significant discounts (up to 51%) on Lilly’s insulin beginning January 1, 2017. Anyone with a prescription for Lilly insulin products can now use the Blink Health platform – which was originally developed to help people receive discounts on generic medications – to directly purchase discounted brand name Lilly insulin. While this program will be particularly beneficial for people with high out-of-pocket costs, high deductible plans, or no insurance, anyone can “buy direct” through Blink Health. In our article, we dive into the ins-and-outs of this program and provide a handy “how-to” guide on using the discount program. While Lilly’s program is a great start to reducing costs, other opportunities lie ahead to make insulin affordable for all.
 
Finally, I’m thrilled to share our latest article on AlterG’s anti-gravity treadmill. This new technology, originally developed by NASA to help astronauts (!), creates a pressurized chamber around user’s legs that helps them become “semi-weightless.” By lessening the burden of exercise and diminishing the risk of injury, early clinical trials have indicated that the treadmill can increase physical activity, improve glucose levels, and lower cholesterol. Our colleague Abigail Dove (we call her Abigail The Great) got to try it at Obesity Week in New Orleans, and she shares first-hand impressions in this article. Plus, AlterG is looking to gather data about the treadmill’s benefits and is providing free access to interested diaTribe readers with diabetes or pre-diabetes. If you are interested in this opportunity, just fill out AlterG’s form here! Wow - PLEASE spread the word!
