ALEXA/HEATHER "philanthropy" event

Alexa Cortes Culwell and Heather McLeod Grant - founders of Open Impact - recently released a report on “The Giving Code: Silicon Valley Nonprofits and Philanthropy.” Their report addressed the “prosperity paradox,” or the widening gulf between the wealthy and working poor in Silicon Valley, which parallels the widening gap between philanthropists and the nonprofits working to serve the working community’s needs. While demand is rising for nonprofits’ services to address homelessness, poverty, and inadequate public education in Silicon Valley, funding for these community-based organizations is diminishing. To put together their report on this issue, Alexa, Heather, and the Open Impact team conducted over 300 interviews and discussions with stakeholders and held multiple feedback sessions with community partners. In “The Giving Code” they tackle the question of how best to connect small nonprofits with Silicon Valley Philanthropists, and how to adequately coordinate this fragmented ecosystem. Here is a link to the full report, and here is a link to the summary of the report.
