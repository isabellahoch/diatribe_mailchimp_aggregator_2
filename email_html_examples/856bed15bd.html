Exciting news! Medicare will now cover CGM use with smartphone apps, including sharing features. Medicare beneficiaries can now use CGM with approved smartphone apps (currently Dexcom’s G5 mobile app). This update will provide greater patient safety and convenience via remote monitoring capabilities, brighter screens, more audible alerts, and the convenience of not having to carry a separate receiver. The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) are expected to update coverage by June 21. This coverage update also bodes well for Dexcom’s G6, which has already been filed with CMS for coverage but hasn't been approved yet – a decision is expected this fall, and we imagine it will be positive. 

In this issue, we dive into one of our most frequently asked questions,“What do I eat if I have diabetes?” with a summary and review of Diabetes UK’s most recent nutrition guidelines. Diabetes UK’s 62-page Nutrition Review provides food-focused practical nutrition guidelines for people with diabetes and prediabetes. Here at diaTribe, we agree with many of the guidelines set by Diabetes UK, and we also recommend going an extra step by reducing carb intake to around 100 grams per day. Diabetes UK is so impressive and we learn so much from them! 

Heart failure is one of the most common and overlooked complications for people with diabetes - before I worked at diaTribe, I didn't know anything about it at all! In our article “Heart Failure – The Overlooked Diabetes Complication, Part 1: What and Why?” we share information on heart failure, its relationship with diabetes, symptoms, and screening.
