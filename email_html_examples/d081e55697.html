Content-wise, we’re also trying to learn more about what does well when, where, and why! There’s some very interesting nuance to how traffic numbers shake out on different platforms. For newer content, the newsletter is still a very significant source of our overall traffic. Facebook, a more “fleeting” driver of traffic, can be hit-or-miss in many instances. Once a promoted piece on Facebook does really well (i.e., many likes, shares, clicks) in a short period of time, it starts to appear for more and more people, and tends to continue to do well. Tech pieces with substantial product updates (e.g., about Medtronic’s MiniMed 670G launch or the accuracy of the Dexcom G6 sensor) tend to drive an unparalleled amount of traffic to our site from Facebook, but not every single Facebook post performs equally as well. And Google drives a relatively small amount of traffic to the site for new pieces; Media Cause has explained that this is because it takes time and traffic volume before pieces earn the “trust” of the search engine algorithm so that they rank more highly in organic searches. This is demonstrated by the fact that our best performing pieces were those written about 1-2 years ago. These are the pieces we’ve worked on with Media Cause to create landing pages and to drive traffic through Google with our AdGrants budget; they’re also the ones that have gained search engine “trust” and rank more highly in searches.
