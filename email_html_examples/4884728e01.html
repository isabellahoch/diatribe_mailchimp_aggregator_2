Dear Dr. Ryan,

Thank you again for taking the time to speak to earlier today, and for being so willing to contribute to our Anthology project. We feel SO lucky to have your invaluable perspective – we've already learned so much from you during just one phone call! I’m writing to recap some details that we talked about over the phone, including what we expect your role to be over the course of the Anthology’s development and what we hope you can contribute over the coming weeks.

Our hopes are that you, as a member of our Expert Panel, would be willing to:
  1. Evaluate the collection of programs that we have assembled for the Anthology’s first section, on prevention, using our evaluation guidelines, as well as provide general feedback and thoughts on each program
  2. Supply additional ideas of great diabetes and obesity prevention programs that we could include in our anthology
  3. Advise us on the best platform to publish this project, and how to increase accessibility/visibility to a broad audience
  4. Review the final drafts of the Anthology before we publish
During our conversation asked briefly  about whether you had any thoughts on two questions: 1) who else we might speak to for insights on prevention programs, including those who might have valuable perspective that are relevant to but fall outside the specific silo of diabetes and endocrinology, and 2) strong examples we might explore in determining how to design our online platform for the Anthology. For example, we really appreciated your suggestions to speak to Rena Wing and Leigh Perreault, and to look into UnitedHealth's Real Appeal program. Let us know if you have any additional thoughts on these topics!

As far as what we hope you can contribute in the near future, our first stage is to establish a group of prevention programs to be featured in the Anthology. We have already created a collection of about 20 summaries of prevention initiatives which we are considering including. This collection is by no means an exhaustive list and we always welcome additional suggestions. We have asked you to rate several aspects of each program that we felt were important in assessing its value as an entry in the anthology. After each program, we have included a corresponding evaluation sheet on which we ask you to assess its outcomes, scalability, potential for impact, sustainability, and inspiration. In an effort to prevent this from being too time-consuming a task, these ratings are primarily in the form of a 0 to 3 ranking, corresponding to “strongly disagree” to “strongly agree.” We’ve also left space for any suggestions that you have which might not relate directly to the questions that we asked. We are grateful for any and all suggestions you might provide. At the end of the document, we’ve also included a few questions regarding your response to the collection and its evaluation as a whole. We especially appreciate any suggestions you might have here on how we might strengthen or refine our inclusion criteria.
