Dear Expert Panelists,

We hope you have had a happy, healthy, and productive start to 2017. We have certainly been busy here at The diaTribe Foundation, and we are looking forward to all of the exciting work and collaboration that the year will bring.
 
Several week ago, we shared with you a completed draft of the Anthology of Bright Spots in prevention of type 2 diabetes – we have aset the ambitious goal of publishing this section, designing and building the web platform for the Anthology, and carrying out subsequent research in other areas of Bright Spots, all in the first half of 2017.
 
Today we are excited to share with you a fully designed print version so that you can get a sense of the presentation. Of course, the Anthology will ultimately be housed as a searchable online collection, but we are hoping to keep the content moving forward as we go through the somewhat slower process of web design and development.
We are still hoping to receive your feedback and advice regarding this section of the Anthology, and we would like to schedule a call with you sometime soon. Please let us know if there are times at which you might be available for such a call in the next 2-3 weeks. You can also schedule a call through this link.
 
We, of course, do not expect you to read the entire, 100-page document, but we do hope that you may be able to glance over it. We most hope for your input on the following: How can we strengthen this project as a whole and how can we sharpen its presentation? What will be the best way(s) to strengthen the prevention program section as well as the insights section? What might we still be missing? Very importantly, what should not be included? We hope that focusing on these bigger-picture questions will make this a more convenient, efficient process for you. Additionally, if you think it might be easier to provide written feedback rather than scheduling a call, just let us know – we are incredibly grateful for suggestions of any form.
Thank you again for your continued guidance and support on this effort, and please let us know if you have any questions. We are excited to continue moving forward on this project, and we know that the progress that we have already made, and that we plan to make throughout 2017, would not be possible without you. We look forward to hearing from you soon.
 
Very Best,
Ben Pallant, Amelia Dmowska, Kelly Close, and the diaTribe team
