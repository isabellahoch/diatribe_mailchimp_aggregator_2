Happy Sunday! I so hope that you are well. It’s hard to believe it’s been a little over a month since we all gathered at d16… time is flying! And, our diaTribe Foundation team is even more enthused than we were as when we left the Garden Court. The magic that you and others created in the room in January injected such inspiration and energy into our work, and our commitment to maintain that momentum moving forward is steadfast. And, we can’t wait for you to be a part of our next steps. We have been hard at work on our operational plan coming out of d16, and we’re excited to share our progress with you:
 
d16 Operational Plan:
Our mission is to activate the ideas from d16 so that collectively we can reduce the societal burden of diabetes. The operating plan, which is presented in both a one-page version and a more detailed word document, includes our ideas about next steps, the role that The diaTribe Foundation is prepared to play, and areas that are opportunities for d16 participants to engage. If you have thoughts or suggestions, we would love to hear your feedback.
 
Event Summary:
Here is a letter about d16 that you can pass on to your networks, if you’d like, for those that want to share more about what we started together. It includes a description of d16, everyone’s role in it, and our plans for next steps. No pressure or expectations on who to send this to, of course, but we had a request for this and wanted everyone to have it.
 
Next up: Consensus of Ideas
We are working on a draft of a “Consensus of Ideas” that came out of d16. After sorting through the dozens of pages of notes produced throughout the event, we have now identified
key insights and the ideas that are the most scalable, investible, and impactful. We are planning to get this to you for your feedback at the end of February. We know how busy you are, but if you’d like to volunteer to give us early feedback before then, please let us know and we'll get you the draft we just finished last week! Your input would be incredibly helpful to us.

Articles of note: Here is some recent news/insights that may be of particular interest to you for public health implications. All of these have been written by our Close Concerns team since we met and many have been or will be in diaTribe, our free patient newsletter, as well:

AACE on CGM (continuous glucose monitoring) - this major professional society met yesterday in Washington and the diaTribe Foundation represented a strong patient voice

JAMA on diabetes screening - We're starting to see more on diabetes prevention, at least a little - good to see the focus by this prestigious medical journal

Omada launches PREVENT for underserved populations -
way to go, Sean! 

Pioglitizone (generic!) may be useful in pre-diabetes - This news was entirely unexpected and the propensity to use this in personalized populations may bode particularly well for reducing both diabetes as well as heart disease - and this is an actual inexpensive approach ! We'd love to see it combined with lifestyle.  

Abbott launches FreeStyle Libre in Australia - this was the amazing technology I showed everyone that I used to test my blood glucose (on my arm!). The diaTribe Foundation would like to see regulatory move faster in the US and will be advocating on this front. 

WHO commission on childhood obesity - As Dr Fran Kaufman says, this is a relatively comprehensive assessment of some key targets that need to be addressed - but so great to hear her say it is time to be more aggressive (this so resonates with all the d16 discussion). 

Do you know any great young women high school looking to learn more about science and public health? Ask them to consider applying to Close Concerns' and The diaTribe Foundation's second Junior STEM Associate Program in summer, 2016 - it won rave reviews last year and we need to continue to motivate the smartest minds into public health. 
 
We look so forward to writing you again in a bit over a week with the Consensus of Ideas. Many thanks to you in the meantime for your focus and help to us.

very best,
kelly
