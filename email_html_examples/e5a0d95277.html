I am SO excited to share a few special previews of our featured articles in the upcoming issue of diaTribe! Here are a few highlights that I think you’ll find particularly compelling…

Diabetes technology is changing so rapidly that it’s hard to keep up with the array of launch dates, device updates, and new functionalities. Just for you, we’ve compiled a handy guide so that all of this information is only a click away. In our overview, you’ll find a timeline of release dates of new insulin delivery systems and glucose monitoring devices (all expected to launch in the US by mid-2017 or earlier)! What’s so different about these devices? Our “Deep Dive” section provides in-depth discussion of new features so that you’ll have a much better sense of what’s the same, what’s changed, and where things are going next. A special thanks to in-house tech experts Adam Brown and Ava Runge for all their tireless work on translating the technology world to us!

Dr. Lois Jovanović, world-renowned researcher and mother with diabetes (and my doctor throughout three pregnancies), shared some essential tips for pregnant women with type 1, type 2, or gestational diabetes – including pertinent information on exercise regimens and fluctuating hormone levels. We keep hearing that there are not nearly enough resources for pregnant women with diabetes and we hope this helps fill a gap! The esteemed Dr. Jovanović addressed some common misconceptions regarding diabetes and pregnancy, insisting that a woman with any type of diabetes can deliver healthy babies as long as she works to maintain tight glucose levels.

Finally, we feature a piece on such an important – and often unaddressed! – topic in diabetes: stigma. Dr. Susan Guzman (co-founder of the Behavioral Diabetes Institute) and our very own Adam Brown presented on this issue at AADE 2016 in San Diego; here is the overview of their talks. You’ll also find a video of Adam’s discussion of dQ&A’s research on the pervasive nature of diabetes stigma, plus his full set of slides from this talk!

I hope you enjoy these features of our full diaTribe letter. Thank you, as always, for your continued support!
