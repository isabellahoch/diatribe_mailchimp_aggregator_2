With a landmark meeting on "Outcomes Beyond A1C" just behind us, It’s been an exciting (and busy!) summer for our team here at The diaTribe Foundation. I’m writing to share a preview the upcoming issue of diaTribe, which includes an overview of this important convening and much, much more!

We would LOVE your help sharing content online. We have included some sample tweets and photos at the bottom of this letter – thank you in, advance, for your help tweeting, posting, and emailing these articles!
 
On July 21, 2017, The diaTribe Foundation convened leaders in the diabetes field to discuss “Glycemic Outcomes Beyond A1C: Standardization and Implementation” and reach a consensus on outcomes such as hypoglycemia, time spent in-range, and hyperglycemia. To read about some of the agreements reached, including standardization of thresholds for measuring glucose levels, acknowledgment of A1C as a misleading measure in individuals, and support of expanded use of CGM in future diabetes research, click here. This landmark meeting would not have been possible without the tremendous work of the diabetes community at large - and in particular, from the DOC, Cherise Shockley of DSMA among many others. If you have been involved in this movement, thank you for your tremendous work to spread the word that we must move #beyondA1C. See our article for next steps. 
 
We just finished the second article in our six-part series on navigating insurance and access. “Diabetes at Work: Employee Insurance, Benefits, and Rights,” covers everything you need to know about employer-based insurance and how to maximize health benefits through your work place. Don’t forget to check out the first part of our series – “When Insurance Gets Turned Down: Appeals Explained” – which explains the appeals process and shares tips for overturning denied coverage. 

Do you have any feedback or suggestions for this series? We would love to share your personal tips and any additional resources related to insurance. We are particularly looking to connect with people we could interview for our future articles in this series (on topics like patient assistance programs, optimizing health care spending, navigating Medicare and government-funded plans, and changing insurance). If you or anyone you know could help us, please let us know!
 
Exciting news from Abbott and Bigfoot Biomedical – the two diabetes technology companies are partnering to automate insulin delivery for pumpers (“hybrid closed loop”) and people on injections “automatic titration”). Bigfoot’s systems will use a next-generation FreeStlye Libre glucose monitoring sensor from Abbott which will not require a fingerstick calibration from a blood sugar.
 
In the latest Adam’s Corner, Adam shares his favorite diabetes food tricks and recipes from Bright Spots & Landmines. In this article, he explains why eating a low-carb, higher-fat diet helps his improve blood sugars and decrease diabetes burden. Plus, he shares how to make delicious low-carb meals, like three-egg veggie scrambles, “nut/seed cereal,” chia seed pudding, almond waffles, and more! 
 
I hope you enjoyed this preview of our full diaTribe issue! Thank you for your continuous support of our labor of love.
yours truly,
