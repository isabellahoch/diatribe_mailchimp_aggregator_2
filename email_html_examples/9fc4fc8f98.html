HIGHLIGHTS FROM THE NEWS:
  • On May 2nd, the FDA began implementing a policy that mandates that restaurants, movie theaters, convenience stores, and other businesses with 20 or more locations that serve food are required to post calorie counts. The new rule has been delayed several times over the past decades, largely due to food industry’s pushback. Read the official statement from FDA Commissioner Scott Gottlieb here.
  • Seattle has collected more than $4 million during the first three months of its tax on sugar-sweetened beverages, syrups, and concentrates (a 1.75 cent-per-fluid-ounce tax). The City Council voted in favor of the tax last June, and many have lauded the move as part of the push to reduce the consumption of sugar drinks. Read the news from the Seattle Times here.
  • What could Jeff Bezos, the richest person in the world, spend $131 billion on? We thought this New York Times opinion piece was very thought-provoking... if you have any ideas, or have a personal connection to Jeff so that we can send him a letter convincing him to invest in some of the incredible programs showcased in the Anthology of Bright Spots, let us know.
  • A study recently published in the Journal of General Internal Medicine indicates that US diabetes screening protocol could miss half of all diabetes/prediabetes cases. The US Preventive Services Task Force (USPTF) recommends screening based on age (40-70 years) and BMI (overweight or obesity), but these limited criteria only identified half of people with diabetes or prediabetes in the study. Even expanded criteria (family history of diabetes, history of gestational diabetes or polycystic ovarian syndrome, and non-white race/ethnicity) missed 23% of prediabetes and diabetes cases. This study underscores the need for expanding comprehensive diabetes screening to include other high-risk subgroups.
